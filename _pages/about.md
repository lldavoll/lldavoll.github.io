<div style="text-align: justify;">

I am an AI/ML Engineer with a Master’s degree in Human Language Technology and a background in Electrical Engineering, specializing in natural language processing, data pipelines, and applied machine learning. My work focuses on building end-to-end systems that transform large, complex, and often unstructured data into structured, actionable insights.

</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
My interest in this field began during the last year of my undergraduate studies in Electrical Engieering in Colombia in ehich I learned about classifiers, machine learning and neural networks, where I developed a passion in how computational methods can model, process, and extract meaning from real-world data. This perspective has guided my work in both academic and industry settings, especially when dealing with noisy, large-scale datasets.
  
</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
During my Master’s program in Human Language Technology, I developed a strong foundation in statistical natural language processing and quantitative methods for linguistic data. I worked with core NLP techniques such as n-gram modeling, document classification, and information retrieval, and applied statistical methods including regression modeling, hypothesis testing, and mixed-effects modeling to analyze linguistic and behavioral data. Through these experience I gained the tools to design experiments, build and evaluate models, and interprete results in a rigorous and reproducible way.
  
</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
During my internship at Goods Unite Us, I developed scalable data pipelines integrating multiple data sources, including SEC filings, web-based data, and company datasets. My work involved extracting executive information from financial filings, resolving company identities across datasets, and linking corporate data to external sources such as political contribution records. I applied NLP techniques for information extraction, normalization, and data quality improvement, and worked with platforms such as Snowflake, AWS, Azure, and Firebase to manage and analyze large-scale datasets. This experience strengthened my ability to bridge raw data and real-world applications by translating analytical outputs into meaningful insights that support decision-making. 
  
</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
In addition to industry experience, I have conducted research involving multilingual corpora, statistical modeling, and machine learning, including building annotated datasets and developing automated preprocessing pipelines in Python and R. My work also extends to experimental and behavioral data, where I have designed and analyzed psycholinguistic experiments using eye-tracking and self-paced listening methodologies, applying mixed-effects models and statistical analysis to understand language processing.
  
</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
My technical toolkit includes Python (pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, NLTK, spaCy, Transformers, PyTorch, TensorFlow), R (tidyverse, dplyr, ggplot2, tidyr, stringr, readr, caret, quanteda), SQL, Linux/Bash, Git/GitHub, Perl, Prolog and cloud-based data platforms. I am passionate in solving real-world problems where data is messy, ambiguous, and large-scale, requiring both analytical rigor and practical engineering solutions.
  
</div>

<div style="text-align: justify;">
I am currently seeking full-time opportunities in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, or Data Analytics where I can contribute to building data-driven systems that create measurable impact. I am especially motivated by work that makes complex data more accessible and usable, helping bridge the gap between raw information and meaningful, real-world decisions.

</div>
