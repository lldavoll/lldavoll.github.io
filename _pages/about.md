I am an AI/ML Engineer with a Master’s degree in Human Language Technology and a background in Electrical Engineering, specializing in natural language processing, data pipelines, and applied machine learning. My work focuses on building end-to-end systems that transform large, complex, and often unstructured data into structured, actionable insights.

During my industry experience, I developed scalable data pipelines integrating multiple data sources, including SEC filings, web scraping, and company datasets. I applied NLP techniques for information extraction, normalization, and data quality improvement, and worked with platforms such as Snowflake, AWS, Azure, and Firebase to manage and analyze large-scale datasets. This experience strengthened my ability to manipulate raw data and transform it into analytical outputs and meaningful insights that support decision-making.

In addition to industry experience, I have conducted research involving multilingual corpora, statistical modeling, and machine learning, including building annotated datasets and developing automated preprocessing pipelines in Python and R. My work also extends to experimental and behavioral data, where I have designed and analyzed psycholinguistic experiments using eye-tracking and self-paced listening methodologies.

My technical toolkit includes Python (pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, NLTK, spaCy, Transformers, PyTorch, TensorFlow), R (tidyverse, dplyr, ggplot2, tidyr, stringr, readr, caret, quanteda), SQL, and cloud-based data platforms. I focus in solving problems where data is messy, ambiguous, and large-scale, requiring both analytical rigor and practical engineering solutions.

I am currently seeking full-time opportunities in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, or Data Analytics where I can contribute to building data-driven systems that create measurable impact.
