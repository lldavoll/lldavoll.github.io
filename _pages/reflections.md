Below, you’ll find my personal thoughts about my journey being an instructor of Spanish as a second language. Feel free to read them and share me your toughts via email.

## About Pragmatics

<div style="text-align: justify;">

<p>Recognizing pragmatics as a <strong>critical dimension of language learning</strong>, I came to understand that effective communication extends far beyond grammatical accuracy to include <em>cultural awareness, context, and social interaction</em>. A pivotal moment in my teaching occurred when a student used the phrase <em>“Repite eso”</em>—grammatically correct, yet pragmatically inappropriate in that context. This instance revealed a gap not in linguistic knowledge, but in <strong>understanding how language functions within cultural norms</strong>.</p>

<p>This experience led me to intentionally design strategies that strengthen students’ <strong>pragmatic competence</strong>. I adopted a multi-layered approach centered on <em>cultural immersion and real-world communication</em>. This includes dedicating class time to comparing communication styles between U.S. and Spanish-speaking contexts, implementing <strong>role-playing activities</strong> that explore varying levels of formality and politeness, and analyzing authentic video interactions to highlight subtle linguistic and cultural cues. Through these practices, students begin to recognize that language learning is not limited to vocabulary and grammar, but involves <strong>interpreting meaning within social and cultural frameworks</strong>.</p>

<p>Ultimately, my goal is to help students develop the ability to communicate <strong>not only accurately, but appropriately</strong>. By fostering awareness of cultural expectations and communicative norms, students gain the tools to navigate Spanish-speaking environments with <em>sensitivity, confidence, and respect</em>. This approach supports the development of a more <strong>holistic communicative competence</strong>, enabling learners to build meaningful connections, avoid pragmatic misunderstandings, and engage with the language in a way that reflects genuine cultural understanding.</p>

</div>

## About Peer Interaction

<div style="text-align: justify;">

<p>I consider peer interaction to be a <strong>fundamental component of the language classroom</strong>, extending beyond academic learning into students’ <em>social and emotional development</em>. To strengthen student connections and promote meaningful communication, I implement <strong>“Passion Talks”</strong> as a structured activity that allows students to present topics of personal interest. These presentations are distributed strategically over a 14-day period, with two students presenting per day, ensuring consistent engagement and participation.</p>

<p>A recurring challenge in this process is maintaining sustained use of the <em>target language (L2)</em>, as students often revert to English due to <strong>pronunciation insecurities and lack of confidence</strong>. Rather than viewing this as a limitation, I approach it as an opportunity to create a more supportive and low-stakes environment for language use. Within this framework, “Passion Talks” serve multiple pedagogical purposes: they <strong>encourage authentic peer interaction</strong>, help students <strong>identify shared interests</strong>, and facilitate the formation of <strong>collaborative learning communities</strong>.</p>

<p>By providing a motivating and personalized platform for communication, this approach contributes to <strong>reducing linguistic anxiety</strong> and fostering greater confidence in speaking Spanish. Although challenges in L2 use persist, “Passion Talks” represent a <em>practical and effective strategy</em> for promoting interaction, engagement, and student-centered learning. Ultimately, this method supports the development of a classroom environment where communication is not only practiced, but <strong>valued as a tool for connection and self-expression</strong>.</p>

</div>
