<div style="text-align: justify;">

<p>I strongly believe that effective language teaching is an <strong>inclusive, collaborative process</strong> in which knowledge is co-constructed through shared experiences in the classroom. Grounded in <em>Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT)</em>, I move away from traditional teacher-centered models and instead design learning environments that place students at the center of their educational journey, where <strong>meaningful communication—not memorization—is the primary goal</strong>.</p>

<p>My communicative approach provides students with sustained opportunities to engage with the target language in authentic ways, fostering both <strong>confidence and creativity in linguistic expression</strong>. By intentionally varying lesson structures to accommodate diverse learning styles and preferences, I aim to transform the classroom into what I describe as an <em>“endless adventure”</em>—a dynamic space where language learning is engaging, personal, and purposeful. One example of this is my use of <strong>“Passion Talks,”</strong> in which students explore topics of personal interest while developing fluency, ownership, and communicative competence.</p>

<p>As both an instructor and a multilingual speaker, I view language learning as extending far beyond the memorization of grammatical rules. It is a process of <strong>engaging with new ways of thinking, understanding diverse cultures, and expanding one’s global perspective</strong>. While I recognize the importance of explicit grammar instruction, I prioritize <em>contextualized learning</em>, presenting grammatical concepts through interactive activities, games, and mnemonic strategies that allow students to <strong>internalize patterns naturally</strong>. Activities such as <em>bingo, gallery walks, and study stations</em> transform grammar instruction into a participatory and meaningful experience.</p>

<p>I conceptualize the classroom as a <strong>shared space of continuous growth</strong>, where both instructor and students actively contribute to the learning process. I encourage students to take risks, negotiate meaning, and navigate communicative challenges as they would in real-world interactions. This approach not only strengthens linguistic proficiency but also cultivates <strong>intercultural awareness and communicative adaptability</strong>.</p>

<p>Ultimately, my role is to design <strong>inclusive, purposeful learning experiences</strong> that empower students to become autonomous language users. I strive to provide the tools, strategies, and environments necessary for students to develop both their linguistic abilities and their confidence as communicators. In this collaborative process, I continuously learn from my students, refining my pedagogy and contributing to a classroom environment that is <strong>dynamic, reflective, and constantly evolving</strong>.</p>

</div>
